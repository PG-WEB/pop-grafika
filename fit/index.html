Collect+Art+Delete:
e-commerce and the art market

James Cohan opened his eponymous gallery in 1999 and currently exhibits established contemporary artists at locations in New York and Shanghai. Cohan recently teamed up with Internet entrepreneur, Jonas Almgren, to launch VIP Art Fair; the first online-only art fair, which will debut in January 2011. Fair participants include a roster of major international galleries.

New York-based collector, Ron Dominguez, has acquired an eclectic array of emerging art. The Dominguez Collection includes early work by Jeff Soto, Jonathan Viner, and Marion Peck, as well as Gary Baseman, The Date Farmers, Liz McGrath, The Clayton Brothers, Souther Salazar, Andy Kehoe, AJ Fosik, David Stoupakis, and others.

David Grosz is editor in Chief of Artifex Press, a recently launched publishing company created to produce and maintain comprehensive, publicly accessible online catalogue raisonnés. Artifex Press LLC was founded in 2008 by Marc Glimcher, president of The Pace Gallery. Its first catalogue projects will be the estates of Sol LeWitt and Agnes Martin, along with contemporary artists Chuck Close, John Chamberlain, Claes Oldenburg and Coosje van Bruggen.

Gracie Mansion is a Contemporary and Modern Art Auction Specialist at artnet.com, a leading online art marketplace and database. She is also an independent curator and consultant to private collectors, corporations and museums. Her experience in the art world began nearly 30 years ago, with the 1982 opening of her gallery in the East Village, after moving to SoHo and Chelsea, she closed her gallery in 2002 to concentrate on private dealing and the secondary market.

Sheri L. Pasquarella is currently Principal of SLP Art Culture and Commerce, an entity devoted to an array of cultural development needs such as art advising, curatorial consulting, art business and not-for-profit development and publications. Prior to establishing SLP in 2005, she worked as a Director for Gorney Bravin + Lee and Marlborough galleries. In 2002, Ms. Pasquarella conceived and co-founded the New Art Dealers Alliance (NADA), a not-for-profit organization that promotes the understanding of contemporary emerging art and holds a fair each year in Miami. Ms. Pasquarella has lectured widely on contemporary art practices and is currently on the faculty of the Art Market MA degree program at the Fashion Institute of Technology.

Over the last 20 years, art galleries, dealers and collectors have used the Internet to experiment with techniques for viewing and buying art, some with more success than others. The emergence of art sales transacted fully online, whether by auction or direct purchase, raises many new questions for dealers, collectors and artists.
Collect + Art + Delete brings together a distinguished art world panel to discuss the effects of e-commerce on the art market. This discussion will address the impact online art sales have on connoisseurship, gallery and auction house functions, transparency, and any shifts in who buys art and for what purpose.

This event is free and open to the public.

Collect+Art+Delete was organized by candidates for the Master of Arts degree in Art Market: Principles and Practices at FIT. In their second year, students take a two-semester course in which they organize a symposium and curate, execute and promote a group gallery exhibition. They leave the program with the knowledge, skills and experience needed for professional careers in the art market. The Fashion Institute of Technology is a selective college of art and design, business and technology of the State University of New York, with 44 majors leading to AAS, BFA, BS, MA, and MPS degrees.
